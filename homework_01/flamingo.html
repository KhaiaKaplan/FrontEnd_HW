Different flamingo species



The word ‘flamingo’ is said to come from the Spanish word ‘flamenco’ meaning fire, which refers to the bright pink or orange colour of the feathers. There are six species of flamingoes. Four species are distributed throughout the Americas and two species are native to Africa, Asia, and Europe. The six species are:

  • the greater flamingo (Phoenicopterus roseus)
  • the Chilean flamingo (Phoenicopterus chilensis)
  • the lesser flamingo (Phoeniconaias minor)
  • the Andean flamingo (Phoenicoparrus andinus)
  • the puna or James’s flamingo (Phoenicoparrus jamesi)
  • and the American or Caribbean flamingo (Phoenicopterus ruber)
